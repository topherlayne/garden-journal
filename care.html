Watering

At planting

Soak the top 4–5 inches completely. Settles soil around seeds and roots, softens the cardboard below.

First two weeks

Water daily or every other day. Surface stays moist but shouldn't puddle. Gentle spray only.

After establishment

Every 2–3 days in spring. Daily in peak July–August heat. Water in the morning. Direct at soil, not leaves.

Diagnosing trouble: Afternoon wilting on a hot day is normal. Still wilted in the morning? Water immediately. Yellow lower leaves + damp soil = nitrogen deficiency from the mulch component, not a water problem.

Fertilizer plan

The garden mix starts with decent nutrition, but the mulch component ties up nitrogen as it breaks down.

Weeks 1–4
Do nothing. Watch for yellowing on tomatoes and brassicas.
Weeks 4–6
Side-dress heavy feeders (tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, okra, cabbage, kale) with blood meal or 10-10-10. Small handful 3–4 inches from stem, scratch in, water.
At fruit set
Side-dress tomatoes and peppers again when small green fruits appear.
Root crops
No extra nitrogen. Bone meal only for phosphorus. Too much N = big tops, tiny roots.
Herbs
Don't fertilize. More flavor when slightly stressed.

Succession sowing + replacements

Some crops finish fast and free up space for a new round. Here's when to resow and what to put in empty squares.

Cilantro: Resow every 3 weeks from April through early June. Stop when daytime temps stay above 85°F. Start again in September.

Bok choy: Sow once now (spring), harvest by late May, replace the square with a warm-season crop (more okra, bush beans, or basil). Sow bok choy again in August for fall harvest.

Carrots + beets: Sow now, harvest June/July. Sow a second round in August for fall.

Daikon: Same as carrots. Spring round now, fall round in August/September.

Green onion: Sow once and keep cutting. They regrow. If you want more, scatter seeds in any bare square whenever you notice one.

Pest watch

Cabbage moths

Target: cabbage, kale, bok choy

Small white butterflies whose caterpillars eat holes in everything. Floating row cover is the fix. If caterpillars get through, BT spray (Thuricide or Dipel) kills them without harming anything else.

Tomato hornworm

Target: tomatoes

Fat green caterpillars, 3–4 inches, perfectly camouflaged. Hand-pick into soapy water. If one is covered in white cocoons, leave it — parasitic wasp eggs.

Cucumber beetles

Target: cucumbers

Small striped/spotted yellow-green beetles. Spread bacterial wilt (no cure). Neem oil early season, hand-pick. Sudden vine wilt = pull the plant.

Aphids

Target: everything

Tiny soft-bodied insects on new growth. Hard spray of water knocks them off. Insecticidal soap if persistent. Ladybugs will find them.

Fire ants

Target: your hands

You treated before clearing. Monitor for new mounds. Spinosad bait on new mounds. Don't let one establish in the bed.
